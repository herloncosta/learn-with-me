# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Software engineers visiting the blog to read in-depth articles on software engineering topics. They come for clear, well-structured technical content — not fluff or hype. Reading on both desktop (during deep work) and mobile (between tasks). They value accessible typography, fast loads, and content-first design.

## Product Purpose

A personal blog about software engineering. Simple, modern, performant, and usability-focused. The design exists to serve the reading experience — nothing more.

## Brand Personality

Technical, minimalista. Quiet confidence — clean spacing, restrained color, content that breathes. Think Dan Abramov's blog or Styling Bits: functional, not decorative. The site should feel engineered, not designed.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS patterns: identical card grids, gradient text, glassmorphism, side-stripe borders, the hero-metric template, tiny uppercase kickers above every section. Also avoid heavy WordPress-like layouts with cluttered sidebars or decorative fonts.

## Design Principles

1. **Content first** — layout, spacing, and motion serve the reading experience. Remove anything that doesn't.
2. **Engineered simplicity** — every component earns its place. Prefer a single well-tuned element over a composed one.
3. **Performance is a feature** — fast loads, small bundles, no unnecessary JavaScript.
4. **Quiet confidence** — the design steps back and lets the writing lead. Restrained palette, generous whitespace, deliberate typography.
5. **Usability as default** — WCAG AA contrast, responsive by instinct, reduced-motion support, keyboard-navigable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: contrast ≥4.5:1 body text, ≥3:1 large text. Keyboard navigation, focus indicators, reduced-motion support. No content gated behind visual effects.
